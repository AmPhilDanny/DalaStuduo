**CHALLENGE BRIEF FOR FELLOWS**

You are invited to take part in the Airtel NextGen Cohort Knowledge Showcase 2.0, a national challenge where you build a practical, working solution to a real problem facing your community. This is the 3MTT learning-by-building philosophy in action: what you build, and the problem it solves, is the measure of your growth, not just course completion.

The Showcase runs simultaneously across all 36 states and the FCT (37 jurisdictions), with prizes awarded to the top three fellows in every single state. This brief tells you everything you need to know to take part.

# **Who Can Take Part**

All fellows in the Airtel NextGen Cohort, across all 36 states and the FCT, are eligible to participate. You take part within your own state, and prizes are awarded at the state level, so you are competing alongside fellows in your own jurisdiction. However, only fellows who;

- **Have been onboarded on DHF (Darey platform) and have completed at least 1 mini project can win prizes.**  
- **Who won devices in the previous edition CAN NOT win again (but you can build on Dala).**  
- **Any fellow who have won any device  within the 3MTT program can not win again (however, you can build on Dala)**

# **Your Challenge**

Build a working, demonstrable solution to a real problem you see and experience in your own environment, your state, community, campus, market, or workplace. Nigeria's challenges look different in different places, and the fellows who live these realities are best placed to solve them.

**Your solution must be a working build that can be demonstrated, not just an idea, mockup, or slide deck.**

# **Choose a Focus Area**

Build within any one of the guiding focus areas below, or propose your own locally relevant problem. Originality and local relevance are rewarded.

| Focus Area | Build For Your Own Environment \- Examples |
| :---- | :---- |
| **1\. Financial Inclusion & Livelihoods** | Mobile wallets, savings or ajo/esusu tools, micro-lending, payment helpers, or financial-literacy apps in your local language, for traders, artisans, farmers, and families around you. |
| **2\. Education & Skills** | E-learning tools, AI tutors, exam-prep or skills-assessment apps,  anything that helps people around you learn and earn, including out-of-school youth in your LGA. |
| **3\. Health & Wellbeing** | Appointment and reminder tools, symptom checkers, health-record apps, or awareness solutions in local languages, addressing clinic distance, records, and maternal/child-health gaps. |
| **4\. Agriculture & Food** | Advisory tools, market-price apps, produce marketplaces, or input-tracking solutions, tackling crop disease, post-harvest losses, and weak links to buyers. |
| **5\. Governance & Public Services** | Citizen-reporting tools, service-information apps, or transparency solutions, for queues, service information, and reporting of bad roads, water, or waste. |
| **6\. Open: Your Own Problem** | **Don't see your problem above? Build it anyway. Clearly state the problem, who it affects, and where. Originality and local relevance are rewarded.** |

 

# **Build With Any Tool You Choose**

There is no mandatory platform. Build with whatever lets you deliver the strongest working solution. Solutions are judged on the problem solved and the quality of the build, not on the tool used.

**Option A:  Dala Studio (fully supported, no cost)**

Dala Studio is an AI-powered, no-code platform built for the African context. It is free for this Showcase, with premium access and dedicated support. Choose it and you get:

•  Premium access: 100 Dala credits per day, multi-language support, and priority technical support

•     Support for local Nigerian languages, Yoruba, Hausa, and Igbo

•     Optimised for mobile and low-connectivity environments

•    Drag-and-drop interface suitable for all technical backgrounds, build and deploy AI apps and agents in hours

**Option B: Any Other Platform You Prefer**

Use any platform or stack you are comfortable with. Useful options by build type include:

•   **No-code / low-code app builders:** Lovable, Bolt.new, Replit, Bubble, Glide, Softr, FlutterFlow, Adalo, Thunkable

•   **AI agents and chatbots:** Dala Studio, OpenAI GPTs / Assistants, Google AI Studio (Gemini), Dialogflow, Botpress, Voiceflow, Flowise, n8n

•  **Web and full-code builds:** HTML/CSS/JavaScript, React or Next.js, Python (Flask, Django, Streamlit); deploy on Vercel, Netlify, Render, or GitHub Pages

•  **Mobile builds:** Flutter, React Native, or no-code tools such as FlutterFlow and Thunkable

•   **Data and dashboards:** Streamlit, Google Sheets \+ Apps Script, Power BI, Looker Studio

*If you choose a non-Dala tool, you are responsible for your own platform access and any associated costs. Free-tier or open-source tools are strongly encouraged so that cost is never a barrier.*

# **How to Participate \- Step by Step**

1. **Choose your theme**: Build for a real problem in your own community. Pick one of the six guiding focus areas (Financial Inclusion, Education, Health, Agriculture, Governance, or your own problem) \-  **or define a locally relevant challenge yourself.** 

2. **Pick your platform**: Choose any tool you're comfortable with. **Dala Studio is free with premium access (100 credits/day) and onboarding support**, but you can use any no-code, AI, web, mobile, data platform or **any other platform peculiar to your track. Sign up on Dala Studio here: [https://3mtt.gebeya.com/](https://3mtt.gebeya.com/)** 

3. **Build your solution**: Create a working, demonstrable build during the window.

4. **Submit before the deadline**: Submit through the central Google Form \[ [https://forms.gle/nXP8zWyrWGBEB4Sx5](https://forms.gle/nXP8zWyrWGBEB4Sx5) \] before **13 July, 11:59 PM**. Include your build link, a description, the theme selected, and a clear demo video. No late submissions   
5. **Showcase & win:**  If selected, present your build to a panel at your state-level physical showcase. Winners (1st, 2nd, 3rd) are announced and prizes awarded the same day.

**What to Submit**

•     **Build link:** A working build link, your solution must be live and demonstrable.

•     **Description:** A short, clear description of the problem and your solution.

•     **Theme:** The focus area your solution addresses.

•     **Demo video:** A clear demo video walking through how your build works.

**How You Will Be Judged**

State Programme Managers assess every submission against five criteria:

| Criterion | What It Means |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Relevance** | How directly your solution addresses the chosen challenge area. |
| **Innovation** | The originality and creativity of your approach. |
| **Functionality** | Whether the solution actually works/demonstrates as intended. |
| **Impact Potential** | The realistic potential to benefit your target community. |
| **Presentation Quality** | The clarity of your build description and demo. |

**Tips to Stand Out**

•  Solve a problem you genuinely understand or experienced, local relevance scores well on relevance and impact.

•    Make sure your build actually works end to end before you record your demo. Build can be from the knowledge within your learning track.

•   Keep your demo video short and clear: show the problem, then show your solution solving it.

•   Build during peak connectivity hours, and consider low-bandwidth-friendly tools if your area has connectivity challenges.

•   Submit early, don't wait for the final hour. No late submissions are accepted after **13 July, 11:59 PM.**
